# Research Methodology, Tools Used & Challenges Faced

## Overview

This document explains the overall research process, tools used, data collection methods, workflow structure, and challenges faced while building this B2B SaaS outbound research repository.

The primary goal of this project was to study:
- modern outbound sales systems
- GTM execution strategies
- AI-assisted prospecting
- founder-led growth
- pipeline generation systems
- SaaS growth frameworks
- sales communication patterns

through real-world operators, creators, founders, and SaaS revenue leaders.

---

# Research Methodology

## Step 1 — Expert Identification

The first stage involved identifying strong experts actively working in:
- B2B SaaS sales
- outbound prospecting
- GTM strategy
- AI sales workflows
- LinkedIn growth
- founder-led marketing
- pipeline generation
- sales enablement

Experts were selected based on:
- industry credibility
- educational content quality
- operational experience
- consistency of insights
- practical SaaS knowledge
- audience engagement
- relevance to outbound systems

The goal was to focus on practitioners and operators rather than purely motivational creators.

---

# Step 2 — LinkedIn Research Collection

Recent LinkedIn posts from selected experts were manually collected and analyzed.

The research focused on:
- outbound communication systems
- SDR workflows
- GTM execution
- AI adoption trends
- prospect engagement
- founder-led content strategies
- operational scaling

Each LinkedIn post was documented using a structured research format containing:
- original post link
- topic
- main insight
- key takeaway
- detailed observation
- relevance to B2B SaaS

This helped maintain consistency across the repository.

# Challenges Faced During Research

## 1. Limited LinkedIn API Access

One of the biggest challenges was limited access to LinkedIn content through public APIs.

Many LinkedIn APIs are:
- restricted
- paid
- rate-limited
- difficult to access

Because of this, much of the LinkedIn research required:
- manual collection
- manual formatting
- structured documentation

---

## 2. Transcript Availability Issues

Not all YouTube videos provided:
- properly formatted captions
- downloadable transcripts
- accurate subtitle timing

Some transcripts required:
- manual cleanup
- restructuring
- formatting corrections

before they could be stored inside the repository.

---

## 3. Large-Scale Content Organization

Managing large volumes of:
- LinkedIn posts
- YouTube transcripts
- outbound observations
- growth frameworks
- AI discussions

required careful folder organization and repository structure planning.

Maintaining consistency across files was important for readability and future scalability.

---

## 4. Identifying High-Quality Experts

Many creators discuss SaaS growth at a surface level.

The research focused specifically on:
- practitioners
- GTM operators
- outbound specialists
- SaaS founders
- sales leaders
- experienced growth operators

rather than generic motivational influencers.

Finding high-signal experts required filtering through:
- low-quality content
- repetitive advice
- generic growth creators

---

## 5. AI Content Saturation

A recurring challenge during research was that many posts and videos discussed similar AI topics without operational depth.

The project therefore prioritized:
- actionable frameworks
- operational insights
- real-world execution examples
- measurable GTM systems

instead of generic AI hype content.
